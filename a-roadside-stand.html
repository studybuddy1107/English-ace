A Roadside Stand

About the Poem

‚ÄúA Roadside Stand‚Äù by Robert Frost is a poignant poem that portrays the dreams and disappointments of a rural roadside vendor. It highlights the gap between city life and rural poverty and the desire of the villagers to share in the wealth they see passing by.

Summary

The poem describes a roadside stand set up by poor villagers along a busy highway. Though the stand tries to attract passing city drivers, the poem reveals the frustration and helplessness of the villagers who want to improve their lives but are ignored by the prosperous travelers.

Key Themes

  • Rural poverty and struggle
  • Urban-rural divide
  • Dreams vs. reality
  • Social inequality

Important Questions

  • What does the roadside stand symbolize?
  • How does the poet portray the city people?
  • What emotions does the poem evoke about rural life?
  • How does the poem highlight social inequality?
