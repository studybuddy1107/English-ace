The Enemy

Summary

Pearl S. Buck’s story is about Dr. Sadao, a Japanese doctor who rescues an injured American soldier washed ashore during World War II—even though that soldier is technically his country’s enemy. Despite pressure from nationalistic colleagues and the threat of disgrace, Sadao follows his medical oath and humanity.

Important Questions

  • Why does Dr. Sadao decide to treat the enemy soldier?
  • How does Dr. Sadao’s sense of duty conflict with his national loyalty?
  • What does the story say about humanism and moral choices?

Key Themes

  • Humanity over nationalism
  • Professional ethics and courage
  • Conflict between duty and prejudice
